Zoo Non-profit Website Redesign

ROLE: Lead figma prototype designer

TEAM: 4 students

COMPANY: Lehigh Valley Zoo

GOAL: Redesign 4 pages of a non-profit website in a mobile context for a specific target audience, following a user-centered design process

Process

Read about process here: https://medium.com/@art014/5120c9121f49

Outcome

A refreshed website that provides booking services, a shopping platform, and information on the training facility and staff.

Final prototype: https://www.figma.com/proto/IW8npxZtCbwU6RNzr3VLBv/Lehigh-Valley-Zoo?node-id=1%3A2&scaling=scale-down&page-id=0%3A1&starting-point-node-id=1%3A2&show-proto-sidebar=1


    CHALLENGES

  • constraint of having about 2-3 weeks to go through entire process
  • Did not conduct usability testing before final demo day which would have been helpful in solidifying design and navigation
  • there were some disagreements on both the low and high fidelity designs and we had to negotiate as a team

    SKILLS I GAINED / LESSONS

  • learned additional prototyping skills in figma
  • Iterative design process and the practice of going back to initial research, adjusting designs in order to say aligned with user goals and converge on the final ideas
  • Working in a team, discussing design decisions in a respectful, productive manner
  • The importance of design evidence an not making assumptions about your user
