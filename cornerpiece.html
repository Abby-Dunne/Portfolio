Cornerpiece® Web App

ROLE:front-end developer and UX designer in MVP1

TEAM:other ux designer, scrum master/lead developer, CCO

COMPANY: EnticEdge (internal company product)

GOAL:Take the company’s current Cornerpiece product (a digital document) and transform it into an interactive web app that each client can customize according to their company.

Process

1. Design and discuss different layouts for pages

I came in mid-way through the design phase, as the team had gone through a few different iterations of wireframes for different pages. The other UX designer and I were to finalize the layouts of the 7 vital web pages for MVP1 (Sign-on, Dashboard, Glossary, Targeted Messaging, Goals & Strategies, Toolbox, and Support). I spent time learning what the goal of those pages were and what was most important to prioritize.

2. Sketch out certain pages

Most of the pages had some sort of initial design they had done already, but I had full reigns of creating the toolbox page. I sketched out several different ideas and versions, and also experienced in figma with those different layouts.

3. Finalize desktop and mobile wireframes on figma “ready for dev” (Finalize colors, decide on layouts, and put in icons)

We decided on final colors and fonts, added in all the icons once they were ready. For the mobile designs, I made some last minute changes to them such adding collapsible, accordion style tabs, a ux principle called progressive disclosure. We decided it would be best for the user to see all of the options first so they can decide which one they want to expand and see versus having to scroll to see the content they want.

4. Learn react.js and develop 4 web pages using git / agile process

Once the designs were finalized, I switched over to the development side. The lead developer created a repository and created several issues for me to tackle, mostly including creating components on the page since React is component-based. I did an initial read-up on what React was as I had never used it before, but most of the learning was on-the-fly as I started developing.

Outcome

I was able to contribute the 4 pages of the web app for MVP1 launch.

    CHALLENGES

  • I had to quickly pick up using react.js and as well as sharpen my html/css skills, and there were definitely some learning curves but I got the hang of it eventually
  • There were times when the wireframe designs would be complex implementations and did not make sense development-wise, so we had to adjust some of the designs. For example, there was a tab system that would have a variable amount of tabs (possibly as high as 10). Since this feature would make it overcrowded, we had to consider a different way of displaying the categories. After discussion, the team and I decided a drop-down menu would be more suitable.

    SKILLS I GAINED / LESSONS

  • learned new ux practices from other ux designers
  • experience using react.js and sharpened my html/css skills
  • using Github and an agile development process outside of an academic environment
  • Really found my place in serving as a bridge between design and development; Because I knew a lot of what was focused on and talked about in the design process and the overall big picture of the app, when in the development process I could be there to clarify ideas and logistics, change designs or make decisions when things didn’t match up.
